Causes and Effects

What are the effects of increased greenhouse gas emissions?

•More intense and/or frequent extreme weather events like droughts, heat waves and hurricanesWet
•regions are wetter, dry regions gets drier
•Rising sea levels due to melting of icebergs and sea ice
•Changes in ecosystems and natural habitats
•Increased facilitation in movement of diseases
•Warmer temperatures means more insects that carries diseases like malaria or zika virus can thrive easily

Where do greenhouse gases come from?

Heat and Electricity Production
•Burning of fossil fuels like oil, natural gas and coal to produce electricity contributes to a quarter of greenhouse gas emissions
Agriculture
•Methane is the greenhouse gas that is produced due to the decomposition of manure and passing of gas from dairy cows
Industry
•This includes the manufacturing of goods and raw materials which is responsible for releasing mostly CO2, but also methane, nitrous oxide, and fluorinated gases
Transportation
•Burning of gasoline and diesel to power the world's transportation system releases mainly carbon dioxide
Commercial & Residential
•Burning of natural gas and oil used for heating and cooking releases mostly carbon dioxide and methane
